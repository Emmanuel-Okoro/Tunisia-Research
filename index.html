Timeline
The 28-Day Revolution
From Bouazizi's act of protest to the fall of a 23-year dictatorship
17 DEC 2010
Mohamed Bouazizi sets himself on fire outside the governor's office in Sidi Bouzid after having his produce confiscated and being publicly humiliated by a municipal official.
17 DEC 2010 — EVENING
Ali Bouazizi films the aftermath on a Samsung phone. Footage uploaded to Facebook and sent to Al Jazeera, which broadcasts it that same evening.
18–25 DEC 2010
Protests erupt across Tunisia. Ben Ali's security forces respond with live ammunition.
4 JAN 2011
Bouazizi dies from his burns. His funeral becomes a mass protest.
14 JAN 2011
Ben Ali flees Tunisia to Saudi Arabia. His name is removed from Enfidha airport the following morning.

CONCLUSION — EMMANUEL EWORO OKORO
Tunisia did not collapse because of the Arab Spring. It collapsed because the systems that claimed to rebuild it — international finance, tourism investment, aviation infrastructure — were designed to extract value, not create it. The revolution removed the dictator. It did not remove the architecture he built.
